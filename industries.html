Sectors & Exposures

Industries Served

When expanding overseas, every industry faces a unique matrix of trade and geopolitical risk. Generalist brokers often rely on standard domestic packages, leaving critical overseas exposures completely uninsured.

We outline the specific risk criteria and global underwriting requirements for sectors navigating high-stakes environments.

Sector Briefing

Mining

Mining operations involve extreme capital density, volatile global commodities pricing, and sites in emerging jurisdictions. Operational risks include remote tailings dam stability, heavy machinery breakdown, and political asset confiscation.

Critical Placements
  • Political Risk (Asset confiscation & violence)
  • Environmental Remediation Liability
  • High-Capacity Machinery Breakdown BI

Sector Briefing

Energy

From oil & gas production to renewable grids, energy assets navigate massive physical hazards and environmental audits. Exposures range from blowout control costs on offshore platforms to gradual contamination liabilities and supply interruption.

Critical Placements
  • Offshore Operator's Extra Expense (OEE)
  • Gradual & Sudden Pollution Liability
  • Cargo Transit & Storage Assets

Technology

Software syndicates, cloud infrastructure, and fintech entities face intangible exposures that standard policies fail to value. These include software negligence causing client outages, intellectual property disputes, and systemic ransomware.

Critical Placements
  • Technology Errors & Omissions (Tech E&O)
  • Cyber Security Extortion & Business Interruption
  • Intellectual Property Protection

Sector Briefing

Manufacturing

Manufacturing depends on precise supply chains and strict quality control. Risks include physical factory damage, specialized component supplier outages, defective product recall lawsuits, and trade payment defaults from core distributors.

Critical Placements
  • Contingent Business Interruption (CBI)
  • Product Recall & Contamination Expense
  • Trade Credit Receivable Portfolios

NGOs

Non-Governmental Organizations operating in active conflict zones, disaster zones, or volatile developing markets face severe duty-of-care obligations. Critical risks include kidnapping, evacuations, and sovereign interference.

Critical Placements
  • Kidnap, Ransom & Threat Response
  • Political Evacuation & Medical Emergency
  • Foreign General Liability & D&O
