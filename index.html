Export & Trade Coverages

Essential protections for overseas trade and international operations that standard brokers overlook.

public

Political Risk

Protects exporters, foreign investors, and international traders holding overseas assets, inventories, or equipment against host government interventions, expropriation, currency blockages, and contract frustration.

Key Exposures Covered
Asset Expropriation & Confiscation Protects against host government nationalization, outright seizure of physical facilities/assets, or creeping regulatory deprivation.
Currency Inconvertibility Covers losses arising from the inability to convert local currency earnings into hard currencies (USD/CAD) or transfer funds out of the host country.
Forced Abandonment & War Damage Reimburses physical asset destruction or the forced abandonment of operations due to active warfare, revolution, civil strife, or sabotage.

security

Kidnap & Ransom (K&R)

Secures international sales teams, supply chain personnel, and overseas executives against foreign extortion, kidnapping, and security threats with immediate professional crisis response.

Key Exposures Covered
Crisis Consulting Fees Covers the immediate deployment and unlimited fees of premier security response firms (e.g., Control Risks) to guide negotiations.
Ransom & Extortion Funding Reimburses cash paid under duress to resolve kidnappings, bodily injury extortion, or threats against physical assets and networks.
Emergency Evacuation & Medical Funds emergency security evacuations, trauma care, psychiatric support, and repatriation for affected personnel and families.

payments

Trade Credit

Protects export accounts receivable portfolios against overseas buyer insolvencies, protracted defaults, and political non-payment or currency transfer restrictions.

Key Exposures Covered
Buyer Insolvency Protects export accounts receivable portfolios against customer bankruptcy, receivership, or liquidation.
Protracted Commercial Default Triggers reimbursement when a customer fails to pay undisputed open invoices within a specified period (e.g., 90–180 days).
Sovereign Debt Non-Payment Covers defaults resulting from host government actions, central bank foreign exchange blockages, or public-buyer breaches of trade contracts.
